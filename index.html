P · 2Domestic Policy

The rules at home.

Domestic policy is the decisions, laws, and regulations a government enacts to manage affairs within its own borders. Some examples from the 2010s are:

  1. The Affordable Care Act (Obamacare) — 2010
  2. Financial Regulation — the Dodd-Frank Act, 2010
  3. Automotive Industry Bailout — helping car companies with money issues
  4. Social Policy & Civil Rights
  5. Tax Policy — tax cut for 95% of working families
  6. Environment & Energy — investing in clean energy projects
  7. Education — focused on expanding grants for student loans
P · 3Foreign Policy

How the U.S. worked with the world.

Foreign policy is a government's ideas and goals for working with other countries.

In 2010, the U.S. foreign policy focused on disagreements with Iraq and Afghanistan as well as Iran, North Korea, and China. The U.S. also worked on trade agreements with other countries.

P · 4Civil Rights

Equal treatment for everyone.

Civil rights are legal protections for citizens by the government for equal treatment and opportunities. In 2010, civil rights in the U.S. focused on equal protections for all citizens. Some examples are:

Health care Anti-discrimination Voting rights
